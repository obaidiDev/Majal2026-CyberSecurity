That's a TLS handshake in miniature. When you open an https:// site, your browser and the server use public-key crypto once to deliver a session key, then switch to fast symmetric encryption for the whole page. The 🔒 padlock is exactly this envelope — asymmetric to move the key, symmetric to move the data. (Real keys are 256-bit; our K is a small number only because our toy RSA fits numbers under 55.)
